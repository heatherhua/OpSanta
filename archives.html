Thanks Again!

Kellie (2009)
I really can't put into words exactly what this has done for my family. All weekend I have been thinking of you guys and what a wonderful gift you have given us (everytime I try and think of words to express my graditude I start to cry).
Our family was so greatful when they walked in with food, some presents, diapers, and wipes. We all were like wow that's alot of stuff. then they said "We have more stuff". I couldn't believe it. So out into the rain they went again and came back with a ton of more items. By that time I tried to be quiet. I knew if I tried to talk or say anything I would cry, and I didn't want my children to see me cry. They even brought a tree for us!
I know they all thought they were doing something nice and I know that made them feel great. What they don't know is by giving to our family they have instilled a great urge with our children to give too! My children could not understand how these people who never met us (I'm crying again) could give so much. I explained to my children that when people help you out in life that you have to pay it foward and do something for someone else in need.
When we were putting away the food the boys were all excited. We have been receiving food from the food bank, crisis house and some churches lately. So they were so excited to see good cereal, mac and cheese, crackers, oatmeal, good soups they like, and snacks like pudding, poptarts and granola bars. The one thing they were soooo excited about was all the baby food. It's so cute to watch them get excited about food for their little brother.
I guess I was just overwelmed by how much they brought. It just made my heart explode with joy. Just seeing the diapers and wet wipes were such a blessing that I'm not sure your group could understand, thinking something as simple as diapers could make us so happy.
Saturday was also my baby's birthday. He turned 1. Later that day when I was cooking dinner, it made me happy to open our cubbards and see,,,,,,,food! It was filled to the max! Thank you for that.
Like I said before, I can't put into quick words what this has done for my family. The presents they brought are sitting under the tree, waiting to be opened on Christmas morning. All the people involved with Operation Santa 09 will be in our thought and prayers on Christmas day and the rest of our lives. Your group and You have gone above and beyond our dreams. We expected so little, and you gave so much. We thank you for giving us the privledge and honor to know you, and to be able to give our children something nice for christmas. You will now and forever be in our hearts and prayers.

I am a single mother and the holidays sometimes are tuff but I have never been in such a hard position, especially with the holidays fast approaching. There was a few special people that were a part of Operation Santa that came to our home just before Christmas and brought my kids and I some very special and thoughtful gifts. Thank you all so much for the thought, time and compassion the team put into providing and making a special Christmas for my family and I.
